Hospital Billing Costs Among Different Health Insurance Providers

DS4200 Final Presentation
Contributors: Hamsa Madhira, Selen Tasatmaz, and Katherine Sluggett

Research Question: How do billing costs vary among hospitals in relation to the insurance provider?

Healthcare has become a highly-contested, highly-politicized topic in the United States due to the notoriously high healthcare costs. Private equity relationships with hospitals, rising inflation, and government policies have only exacerbated this problem. Many factors may contribute to the final medical bill issued, including the specifics of hospital, condition, and insurance provider. In this project, we investigate how billing costs for identical medical conditions correlate to these variations.



Introduction to the Data

  • This dataset was sourced from Kaggle and displays synthetically-derived healthcare data.
  • The synthetic patient healthcare records were generated using Python's Faker library to simulate real-world hospital data, thus avoiding HIPAA or privacy violations.
  • The cleaned, processed dataframe is 55,500 x 21 and includes patient demographics, conditions, and billing information.
  • Data types include a range of continuous (numeric), discrete (numeric), and categorical values.

Limitations

The data is synthetic and may not always reflect real-world patterns. The data also does not specify location of these arbitrary hosptials (rural, suburban, urban), which is likely a major indicator of the how high the billing costs are. The state of the hospital's location is also not included, which is also another indicator of cost.
