Visualization 2: Side-by-Side Scatter Plots (static)





Key Takeaways

  • Billing amounts are spread widely across all providers for both male and female patients, with no clear concentration at a specific price point indicating high individual variability that demographic factors alone don't explain.
  • The distributions for male and female patients are nearly mirror images of each other across all five providers, suggesting that gender is not a meaningful predictor of billing cost in this dataset.
  • No single insurance provider stands out as consistently cheaper or more expensive; all five show similarly wide ranges from near-zero to ~$50,000, pointing to within-provider variability as a larger factor than provider choice.
