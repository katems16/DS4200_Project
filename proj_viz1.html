Visualization 1: Stacked Bar Chart -Total Billing by Hospital, Stacked by Provider (interactive)





Key Takeaways

  • Billing totals vary substantially from hospital to hospital for the same medical condition, suggesting that the institution itself (not just the condition) is a significant driver of cost.
  • No single insurance provider consistently dominates billing at every hospital. This mix of providers differs by facility, meaning patients' out-of-pocket expense are highly dependent on where they receive care.
  • Certain condition-hospital combinations show a heavily skewed provider contribution (e.g., one insurer accounting for a disproportionate share of total billing), which could reflect patient population differences or negotiated rate disparities between that provider and that hospital.
